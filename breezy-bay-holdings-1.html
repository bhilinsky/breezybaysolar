Hybrid Energy Architecture

Charged by Free Energy.
Backed by Everything Else.

Our hybrid systems don't rely on a single source. They're designed to accept power from any available input — solar, wind, hydro, shore power, grid, or generator — and intelligently route it through your battery bank so you always have power. Free sources get priority. Paid sources only kick in when needed.

This multi-source architecture means your battery stays full longer, your generator runs less (or gets removed entirely), and your fuel bills plummet. Whether your property has a creek, a windy ridge, or just great sun exposure, we'll build a system that harvests every free watt available to you.

⚡ No Free Energy Available? We've Got You Covered.

Not every property has ideal solar exposure or wind. If free renewable sources aren't available or sufficient at your location, our hybrid inverters intelligently manage the following fallback options so you're never left without power:

Battery-to-battery charging — charge a spare bank from your vehicle alternator while commuting, arrive home with a full battery ready to go
Grid-tied mode — use utility power at lowest possible draw, with battery acting as buffer
Generator integration — auto-start when batteries dip below threshold, charge fast and shut off
Shore power input (marine) — plug in at the dock and top off your bank overnight
Dual-source charging — combine grid + any renewable simultaneously for fastest recharge
Time-of-use optimization — draw from grid only during off-peak low-rate hours

How It Works

DC-Coupled Bypass Architecture

Our systems use a DC-coupled hybrid inverter that sits between your solar panels, battery bank, and your existing panel. Critical loads — refrigerator, lights, HVAC, well pump — are rerouted through the inverter's output. This means they run on solar and battery power first, 24/7. Only if batteries dip below threshold does the system pull from the grid through your small service panel. Your panel's amperage limit stops being the bottleneck.
